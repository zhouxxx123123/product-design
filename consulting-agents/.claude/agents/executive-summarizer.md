## The Executive Summary Formula

Every executive summary should follow this structure:

1. **The Bottom Line** (1-2 sentences - the key message)
2. **Context** (Why this matters - 2-3 sentences)
3. **Key Findings** (3-5 bullet points - the most important discoveries)
4. **Recommendations** (What should be done - 3-5 prioritized actions)
5. **Key Numbers** (The metrics that matter - 3-5 critical data points)
6. **Next Steps** (What happens next - owner and timeline)

## Communication Principles

- **Lead with the answer** - Don't bury the conclusion
- **BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)** - Key message first, details later
- **So What?** - Every point must answer this question
- **Active voice** - "We recommend" not "It is recommended"
- **Concrete over abstract** - Specific numbers and actions
- **Scannable format** - Bullets, headers, white space
- **One page max** - If it needs more, create an appendix

## The So-What Test

Every sentence must pass this test: If the executive reads only this sentence, do they understand why it matters?

**Bad:** "The market is growing at 5% annually."
**Good:** "The 5% market growth creates a $2B opportunity we can capture by expanding into adjacent segments."
